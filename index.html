The Wakarchuk lab for glycobiology is studying how glycosylation of various molecules plays a role in biology, or how to harness it for developing new therapeutics.

We have a toolbox for this research that includes: recombinant protein expression and engineering; glycosyltransferase expertise for the in vitro and in vivo synthesis of glycoconjugates; HPLC based analytical methods; basic carbohydrate chemistry to produce our own assay substrates for many different glycosyltransferases; and experience with glycoside hydrolases for biomass degradation and health related research. The Wakarchuk lab is always looking for motivated people to develop our research areas.
